What Credentials Should a Legitimate Life Insurance Agency Have?

A legitimate life insurance agency must hold a state-issued producer license in every state where it sells policies, plus errors-and-omissions insurance.

Verify any life insurance agent's license through the National Insurance Producer Registry before signing an application.

Before you place coverage through any agency — including Guardian Protection — confirm the following:

  • State producer license: verify on the National Insurance Producer Registry
  • Carrier appointments: the agency should disclose which carriers they're authorized to place business with
  • Errors & omissions (E&O) coverage: standard professional liability for agents
  • Designations (optional but useful): CLU (Chartered Life Underwriter — theamericancollege.edu), ChFC, or LUTCF
  • State complaint history: search your state insurance department's complaint database

Under most state insurance codes — for example, Georgia's O.C.G.A. § 33-23 — selling life insurance without an active license is a misdemeanor (source: oci.georgia.gov).

Pre-Purchase Verification Checklist

  1. Confirm the agent is licensed in your state via NIPR.
  2. Ask which carriers will be quoted and request the comparison in writing.
  3. Verify the carrier's AM Best financial-strength rating (A- or better preferred).
  4. Read the hazardous-duty / aviation / military exclusions carefully.
  5. Confirm conversion privileges if buying term (right to convert to permanent without re-underwriting).
  6. Check the free-look period in your state — typically 10 to 30 days.
  7. Compare any private quote against SGLI/VGLI (veterans) or association group plans (teachers, fire/police unions).
  8. Get the final illustration in writing before signing.

Myths and Facts About Specialty Life Insurance

Myth: Veterans with a VA disability rating cannot get private life insurance.

Fact: Many carriers underwrite veterans with service-connected disabilities at standard rates, depending on the condition.

Myth: First responders always pay surcharged rates.

Fact: Several carriers waive hazardous-duty surcharges for credentialed full-time fire, police, and EMS personnel.

Myth: Teachers don't need extra life insurance because pensions cover survivors.

Fact: Many state teacher pensions reduce survivor benefits by 30–60% and may have offsets with Social Security.

Myth: No-exam policies are always more expensive.

Fact: For healthy applicants under 50, no-exam and exam-based rates are now often within 5–10% of each other.
